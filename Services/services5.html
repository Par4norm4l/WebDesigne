Adobe Photoshop

Adobe Photoshop ([əˈdəubɪ ˈfəutəʃɔp], Edo'ubi Photoshop), or simply Photoshop , is a graphical editor released by Adobe Systems . This product is currently one of the leaders in the field of two-dimensional computer graphic editors in the commercial field and is the most well-known program among Adobe applications. Its latest stable release, Photoshop CC 2015.1 (Creative Cloud), was released on November 30, 2015.


In 1987 , Thomas Knoll, a PhD student at the University of Michigan, began writing a program on a Macintosh to display grayscale images on a monochrome display. This program was called the Display, which caught the attention of Thomas Connolly's brother John Connolly, an employee at Industrial Light & Magic, who advised Thomas that the program should work well. Thomas dropped out of training for six months in 1988 and began working with his brother. They created a program and named it ImagePro. Later, Thomas renamed the program Photoshop. That same year, John embarked on a journey through Silicon Valley and presented the program, introducing it to Apple Computer Inc. and Russell Brown, Adobe Art Director.
